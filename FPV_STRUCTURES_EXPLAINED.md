# FPV Structure Types: Pontoon vs Flexible

## 🚢 PONTOON STRUCTURES (Rigid Platform)

### What It Is:
- **Solid, floating concrete or plastic platforms** anchored to the bottom or edges
- **Heavy-duty, boat-like design** that supports solar panels
- Typically made of:
  - Concrete blocks or polymer floats
  - Aluminum or steel frames
  - Rigid panel mounting brackets

### Key Characteristics:
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Durability** | 25-30 years (very stable) |
| **Construction** | Pre-fabricated modules assembled on water |
| **Movement** | Minimal - stays in fixed position |
| **Wave Tolerance** | Handles moderate waves (0.5-1m) |
| **Cost** | **₹45-60 lakhs per MW** (Higher upfront) |
| **Maintenance** | Easier - stable platform to work on |
| **Installation** | 3-4 months (faster setup) |

### Best For:
- ✅ Large dams (>500 MW capacity)
- ✅ Calm water conditions
- ✅ Long-term projects (20+ years)
- ✅ High-capacity installations
- ✅ Investor-grade projects (proven track record)

### Real-World Example:
**Indira Sagar Dam** (1000 MW):
- Pontoon system: **Fixed 10% coverage**
- Estimated FPV Capacity: **1.5 MWp**
- Why: Stable platform perfect for large installations

## 🌊 FLEXIBLE STRUCTURES (Dynamic Platform)

### What It Is:
- **Moving, cable-suspended solar panels** that flex with water movement
- **HDPE (plastic) cables/ropes** support the panels
- Panels move up & down with water level changes
- Like a **floating raft without rigid anchors**

### Key Characteristics:
| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Durability** | 15-20 years (handles stress better) |
| **Construction** | Simple modules connected with cables |
| **Movement** | High - follows water surface |
| **Wave Tolerance** | Handles rough waves (1-2m+) |
| **Cost** | **₹30-40 lakhs per MW** (Lower upfront) |
| **Maintenance** | **More challenging** - moving platform |
| **Installation** | 2-3 months (faster assembly) |

### Best For:
- ✅ Small to medium dams (<200 MW)
- ✅ Dynamic water level changes (monsoon season)
- ✅ Rough water conditions
- ✅ Budget-conscious projects
- ✅ Pilot/demonstration projects
- ✅ Testing locations

### Real-World Example:
**Koyna Dam** (1960 MW):
- Flexible system: **More adaptable**
- Water level varies 40-50m annually
- Why: Flexible handles reservoir drainage without damage

## 📊 COMPARISON TABLE

| Factor | Pontoon | Flexible |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **Water Level Change** | Handles 2-3m | Handles 20-40m+ |
| **Installation Cost** | HIGH (₹50-60 lakhs/MW) | MEDIUM (₹35-45 lakhs/MW) |
| **Operation Cost** | LOW (stable) | HIGHER (more maintenance) |
| **Panel Safety** | Safer (rigid) | Very Safe (absorbs stress) |
| **Space Requirement** | 50% coverage max | 30-50% coverage typical |
| **Wind Resistance** | Good | Excellent (flexible) |
| **Wave Handling** | 0.5-1m waves | 1-2m+ waves |
| **Lifespan** | 25-30 years | 15-20 years |
| **Best Location** | **Stable reservoirs** | **Monsoon regions** |
| **Capacity** | Large systems | Medium systems |

## 🔧 Technical Details You Need

When you run "Run Analysis" with FPV parameters:
- **Structure Type dropdown** = Choose pontoon or flexible
- **Panel Efficiency** = Affected by structure vibration
  - Pontoon: 18-20% (stable → higher efficiency)
  - Flexible: 15-18% (movement → slight loss)
- **Performance Ratio** = Operating quality
  - Pontoon: 75-80% typical
  - Flexible: 70-75% typical (conservative)
